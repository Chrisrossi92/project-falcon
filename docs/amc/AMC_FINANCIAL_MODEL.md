# AMC Financial Model

## Purpose

The AMC Financial Model defines the financial lens needed for AMC Operations Mode.

AMC finances differ from internal production revenue and should be planned before implementation.

## Internal vs AMC Financial Lens

Internal Operations:

- Fee
- Appraiser split
- Internal production revenue

AMC Operations:

- Client fee
- Vendor fee
- AMC margin

## Client Fee

Client fee is the amount charged to the client for the order.

Client fee supports client reporting, revenue visibility, and margin calculation.

## Vendor Fee

Vendor fee is the amount paid or expected to be paid to the vendor company or vendor appraiser.

Vendor fee may affect assignment decisions, margin analysis, vendor reporting, and future financial approvals.

## AMC Margin

AMC margin is the difference between client fee and vendor fee.

Example:

- Client Fee: $2,000
- Vendor Fee: $1,600
- AMC Margin: $400

## Reporting

AMC reporting should eventually include:

- revenue by client
- vendor fees
- margin by order
- margin by client
- margin by vendor
- margin by product
- turn-time and margin comparisons

## Permissions

Financial visibility should be permission-aware.

Not every AMC user should necessarily see margin, vendor fee, or client fee details. Future permissions may distinguish operational assignment access from financial analytics access.

## Future Expansion

- margin forecasting
- fee approval workflows
- vendor fee negotiation history
- client pricing rules
- product-based pricing
- profitability dashboards
- export/accounting integration
